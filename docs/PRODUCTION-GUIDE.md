# Invicta — Visual Production Guide

**The creative blueprint for every image, film, and motion asset on invicta.com.**
Hand this to a photographer, a videographer, a motion designer, or an AI image
pipeline. It defines the through-line first (so everything feels like one brand),
then the exact shot list for all 20 pages.

> Positioning guardrail — read before shooting anything.
> Invicta is **the human layer behind customer experience**. It is **not** a BPO,
> not a "call center", not an outsourcer. Every frame must say _specialists,
> operations, craft, trust_ — never _cubicles, headsets-and-thumbs-up, phone bank_.
> If a shot could appear in a generic "outsourcing" ad, it is wrong.

---

## 0. How to use this document

- **Section 2 is law.** Grade, lighting, lens, casting and motion are defined
  once, globally. Page briefs (Section 3) only specify what is _unique_ to that
  page — always shoot it _through_ the global direction.
- **Every page brief ends with a copy-paste AI prompt.** Append the Global Style
  Suffix (§2.13) and the Global Negative Prompt to any of them.
- **Priorities and sequencing** are in Section 5 — shoot the hero tier first.

## 1. Creative North Star

**"The calm behind the moment."**

Invicta shows up at the hardest, most human moments — a frozen card, a delayed
payout, a stranded traveller, an anxious patient — and answers with a person.
The visual world must feel like the _inside_ of a world-class operation: composed,
expert, quietly intense, deeply human. Think **the editorial of a luxury
magazine × the candor of a documentary × the restraint of Apple.**

Three words that must be true of every asset: **Human. Composed. Premium.**

Emotional arc the imagery must carry across a scroll:

> Opening scene → Tension (the hard moment) → The person who steps in → The
> operation behind them → Proof → Trust → Invitation.

## 2. Global Art Direction

### 2.1 Photography principles

**Always**

- Real specialists mid-work — reading, listening, thinking, resolving. Caught,
  not posed.
- Natural micro-expressions: focus, empathy, quiet satisfaction, concentration.
- One human truth per frame. A single subject or a small, believable pod.
- Generous negative space. Let the person breathe inside the frame.
- Environmental storytelling — screens, notes, coffee going cold, a lanyard, the
  glow of a monitor — but never clutter.
- Skin, texture, and imperfection kept real. No plastic retouching.

**Never** (instant reject)

- Stock-photo posing; direct-to-camera grins; the "diverse team laughing at a
  laptop"; handshakes; thumbs-up; headset-and-point.
- Rows of identical cubicles; a literal "phone bank"; call-center clichés.
- Neon RGB gamer-den lighting (even on Gaming — see §3.2).
- Fake screens, lorem-ipsom UI, obviously-composited dashboards.
- HDR over-processing, heavy vignette gimmicks, tilt-shift toys.

### 2.2 Casting, wardrobe & expression

- **Casting:** real range of age, ethnicity, and gender — never tokenised, never
  a "casting-call" line-up. Believable specialists, not models. Faces with
  intelligence and warmth.
- **Wardrobe:** elevated-casual, muted and desaturated — charcoal, ink, stone,
  ivory, oat, deep green. **One** wardrobe item per campaign may carry a whisper
  of crimson (a scarf, a knit) but never a uniform-red. No loud branding, no
  logos, no busy patterns.
- **Expression direction:** "You've just solved something quietly and you know
  it." Or: "You're listening harder than the person on the other end expects."
  Never "smile for the camera."

### 2.3 Locations & set

- Real, characterful workspaces: warm modern operations floors, editorial
  home-office corners, glass-walled review rooms, a night-lit ops centre.
- Depth in the room: foreground objects, a mid-ground subject, a soft
  out-of-focus background of the wider operation.
- Surfaces: matte woods, brushed metal, warm concrete, linen, paper. Avoid
  glossy corporate boardrooms and sterile white call-floors.

### 2.4 Color & grade

Anchored to the brand tokens:

| Token   | Value                            | Role in image                                                                                                   |
| ------- | -------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Crimson | `#B4121B` (oklch 0.517 0.202 27) | **One** controlled accent per frame — a light source, a garment thread, a UI highlight. Never floods the frame. |
| Ink     | warm near-black (oklch 0.175)    | Shadow point. Warm, never pure black.                                                                           |
| Ivory   | warm off-white (oklch 0.988)     | Highlight point. Warm, never clinical white.                                                                    |

**Grade recipe (stills & video):**

- Warm shadows (ink), warm highlights (ivory), gently desaturated midtones.
- Skin kept natural and healthy — grade the _world_, not the person.
- A single crimson note allowed to glow; everything else muted around it.
- Subtle film emulation: fine grain, soft highlight roll-off, a hair of halation
  on speculars. **No** teal-orange blockbuster look, **no** crushed blacks.
- LUT direction: "warm editorial / low-contrast filmic," ~contrast 0.9, saturation
  0.85, lifted-but-warm shadows.

### 2.5 Lighting language

- **Primary look:** available light, shaped. One soft key (window / large source),
  a gentle fill, controlled falloff into warm shadow.
- **Screen light as motivation:** monitor glow is a legitimate key — a cool-warm
  wash on a focused face reads as "the work." Use it, don't fear it.
- **Chiaroscuro for drama** (Gaming, Fraud, FinTech, VIP): deeper shadow, a single
  raking source, a crimson practical in the background bokeh.
- **Soft & bright for warmth** (Healthcare, Recruitment, SaaS, Careers): higher
  key, airy, ivory-forward.
- Never flat, never fluorescent-green office overheads, never on-camera flash.

### 2.6 Lens & camera language

- **Format:** cinema/full-frame. Shoot stills and motion on the same bodies for a
  unified look (e.g. ARRI Alexa / Sony Venice for film; Leica SL / Sony A1 for
  stills).
- **Primes only.** 35mm (environmental, the operation), 50mm (the human default),
  85mm (portrait intimacy, isolation).
- **Shallow but not gimmick DOF** — f/1.8–f/2.8. Subject sharp, world softened.
- **Angles:** eye-level for trust; a touch below eye-line for the quiet-hero
  register; over-shoulder to put the viewer _in_ the moment; wide establishing
  for the operation. Avoid gratuitous Dutch tilts and drone gloss.
- **Movement (video):** slow, motivated. Slider push-ins, a gentle handheld
  breath, a slow rack-focus. Never frantic. Everything at a confident, unhurried
  pace — the pace of someone in control.

### 2.7 Composition & framing

- Editorial asymmetry: subject off-centre, intentional negative space for type to
  live in (the site overlays serif headlines — leave room, usually left or lower
  third).
- Layered depth: foreground / midground / background always readable.
- Rule-of-thirds as default; break it deliberately for the big "statement"
  moments (dead-centre, symmetrical, monumental).
- Leave a **quiet zone** in ~30% of frames for UI/type overlay. Note the safe
  side per page (most heroes overlay copy on the left / lower-left).

### 2.8 Video & motion-picture treatment

- **Loops, not ads.** 8–14s, seamless, no hard cut at the loop point (design the
  first and last frame to match, or cross-dissolve the seam).
- **One idea per loop.** A single gesture: a specialist looking up as a message
  lands; hands moving over a keyboard; an ops floor breathing at night.
- **Muted by default.** The homepage hero is the only place with optional sound
  (already wired with a toggle). Everything else is silent ambience.
- **Grade identical to stills** (§2.4). 24fps for the filmic beats; 30fps only for
  UI-screen capture inserts.
- **Motion restraint:** slow, weighted, cinematic. If it feels like a product ad,
  slow it down 30%.
- **Background videos** sit _behind_ a dark scrim + grain (the site already layers
  this) — shoot them darker and lower-contrast than a standalone film so type
  reads on top.

### 2.9 Motion language (GSAP + Motion.dev)

The site's motion system is built; this defines the _intent_ behind it so
storyboards match the code.

- **GSAP owns the scroll story** — masked line reveals, parallax on hero media,
  the scroll-scrubbed "process rail," count-up stats, section hand-offs. Motion
  should _reveal information_ and _guide the eye down the page_, never decorate.
- **Motion.dev owns micro-interaction** — magnetic CTAs (pointer devices only),
  hover lifts, the animated nav underline, the full-screen menu stagger.
- **Lenis** sets the pacing — smooth, weighted, slightly heavy. Every storyboard
  below assumes this calm cadence.
- **Reduced-motion:** every effect degrades to a clean fade / instant end-state.
  Storyboards must have a static-readable version.
- **Three.js:** reserved for Homepage + Gaming only. Never decorative elsewhere.

Reusable scroll beats (reference these by name in page storyboards):

- **MASK-LINE** — serif headline wipes up line-by-line from behind a clip mask.
- **PARALLAX-BED** — hero background drifts slower than the foreground on scroll.
- **RAIL-SCRUB** — a crimson vertical rail fills as a process/timeline scrolls.
- **COUNT-UP** — a stat animates from 0 to value when it enters view.
- **CROSSFADE-HANDOFF** — a dark section fades into a light one (chapter change).
- **IMAGE-SCALE** — a full-bleed image eases from 1.08→1.0 as it settles into view.
- **STICKY-HOLD** — media pins while adjacent copy scrolls through it (editorial).

### 2.10 UI overlays, data & graphic language

- Overlays are **quiet, glass, and real** — a frosted chip, a thin hairline, a
  small live-dot, a single count-up stat. Never a fake "dashboard" collage.
- Data shown must be defensible: SLA targets, coverage, QA standard — the same
  honest metrics the copy uses (never invented client numbers).
- Type in overlays: Geist Mono for labels/eyebrows (tracked-out, uppercase),
  Fraunces for any large number or pull-quote.
- The crimson live-dot (pulsing) is the signature UI motif — "a person is here."

### 2.11 Icon & illustration system

- **Icons:** Lucide, 1.5px stroke, rounded joins, currentColor. Line-only, never
  filled, never duotone. Used sparingly — labels and affordances, not decoration.
- **Illustration:** the house style is **not** flat vector characters. Where a
  diagram is needed (process, ecosystem), use **editorial line diagrams** — thin
  ink strokes on ivory, one crimson accent, generous space, Mono labels. Feels
  hand-drafted by an architect, not a SaaS mascot kit.
- **No** 3D blobs, no gradients-on-icons, no emoji, no isometric clip-art.

### 2.12 Technical delivery specs

**Stills**

- Capture: RAW, min 24MP, 3:2 native.
- Delivery crops per placement (the build uses these aspect ratios):
  - Hero background / full-bleed: **16:9** and a **4:5** mobile crop.
  - Editorial portrait: **4:5**.
  - Standard media block: **4:3**.
  - Avatar / author: **1:1**.
- Master: 16-bit TIFF, graded. Web: AVIF + WebP + JPG fallback, sRGB.
- Long edge ≥ 3000px master; responsive exports at 640 / 1024 / 1600 / 2400px.
- Optimised hero target **< 300KB**; inline media **< 150KB**.

**Video**

- Capture: 4K, 24fps (filmic) / ProRes.
- Delivery: 1920×1080 landscape **and** 1080×1350 vertical (mobile hero).
- Encodes: H.265/HEVC + VP9/WebM, plus H.264 MP4 fallback. Muted track stripped
  (except homepage hero master, which keeps optional audio).
- Length 8–14s seamless loop. Hero target **< 3MB**, ambient loops **< 2MB**.
- Poster frame exported for every loop (first-paint LCP image).

**Naming**
`invicta_<page>_<slot>_<ratio>_v01.<ext>` — e.g.
`invicta_fintech_hero_16x9_v01.avif`.

### 2.13 AI generation workflow

For an AI pipeline (Midjourney / Firefly / Flux / SDXL / Runway-Gen for video):

**Global Style Suffix** — append to every page prompt:

> — shot on ARRI Alexa with 35mm & 85mm cinema primes, f/2, natural available
> light shaped with one soft key and warm falloff, shallow depth of field;
> cinematic editorial color grade: warm ink shadows, ivory highlights, muted
> desaturated midtones, a single controlled crimson accent; documentary candor,
> real unposed specialist mid-work with a natural micro-expression; luxury
> magazine composition with generous negative space for typography; fine film
> grain, soft highlight roll-off; photorealistic, 4K, ultra-detailed skin texture.

**Global Negative Prompt** — append to every generation:

> stock photo, posing for camera, fake smile, direct eye-contact grin, handshake,
> thumbs up, headset thumbs-up, call center cubicles, phone bank, rows of desks,
> RGB neon gamer lighting, teal-and-orange, HDR, oversaturated, plastic skin,
> beauty-retouch, logos, watermark, text, lens flare gimmick, tilt-shift, clip-art,
> 3d render, cartoon, illustration, extra fingers, distorted hands.

**Consistency:** lock a seed / style reference per page; generate a small "cast"
of 4–6 recurring specialist faces and reuse them across pages so the operation
feels like one real company. For video, generate the graded still first, then
image-to-video with a single slow motivated move (push-in / rack-focus).

  sound toggle — replace the current footage with this master.)_

  believable specialist per industry (gaming, fintech, healthcare, travel, saas,
  recruitment, e-commerce, community). 4:5, 85mm, eye-level, quiet confidence.
  These recur across the site.
- **Operations photography.** One wide establishing frame of a warm, modern ops
  floor at golden hour — layered depth, not a call-centre grid.
- **Video loops (ambient).** 3–4 micro-loops for the cinematic chapters: hands
  over a keyboard; a rack-focus from screen to face; a night ops floor; a warm
  over-shoulder of a chat being answered.

  and real. Pulsing crimson live-dot.
- **Motion / scroll storyboard.** Cold-open = the conversation cards typing in and
  cross-fading over the network + hero video (built). Then chapter cadence:
  MASK-LINE headlines → PARALLAX-BED on chapter media → COUNT-UP on the proof band
  → RAIL-SCRUB not used here → CROSSFADE-HANDOFF between dark/light chapters →
  IMAGE-SCALE on the case-study frame → dark INVITATION close.
- **Mood:** intimate, cinematic, quietly proud. **Lens:** 50/85. **Grade:** the
  darkest, most filmic on the site.
- **AI prompt (hero still → i2v):** _"A customer-support specialist in her 30s at a
  warm modern operations desk at dusk, lit by the cool-warm glow of her monitor
  and a soft window behind, mid-listening with a focused, empathetic expression,

## 5. Production sequencing & priorities

Shoot in value order — the site works with placeholders today, so lead with what
changes the first impression most.

1. **Tier 1 — the cast + hero stills (unblocks every page).**
   Cast the 6–8 recurring specialists; shoot every page's **hero still** in one
   or two production days sharing lighting/wardrobe. This alone transforms the site.
2. **Tier 2 — the two hero films.** Homepage + Gaming hero video loops (highest
   emotional leverage; homepage video slot is already wired).
3. **Tier 3 — operations + secondary stills.** The wider operation, secondary
   moments, About/Careers culture, leadership portraits.
4. **Tier 4 — editorial system.** Insights lead images + author portraits + the
   house line-diagrams.
5. **Tier 5 — ambient video loops** for the cinematic chapters.

**One production, one look.** Shoot stills and motion together, same cast, same
grade, same day where possible — that continuity is what makes it feel like one
real company rather than a stock library.
