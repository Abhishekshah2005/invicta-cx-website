# Invicta — Phase 1: Discovery, Strategy & Website Blueprint

_Prepared as an agency-grade strategic proposal. This document is the reference
of record for Phases 2–7. Nothing here is built yet — it is the plan we build
against._

**Sources synthesized:** the Invicta player-support & VIP source document
(primary source of truth), the Invicta brand mark, and a competitive read of
intouchcx.com and hugoinc.com (for pattern analysis only — not to copy).

---

## 0. Executive Summary — The Strategic Thesis

Invicta is not a BPO and must never look like one. The brand mark tells the real
story: **"Invicta" is Latin for _unconquered / invincible_**, rendered as a
flame-maned crimson **warhorse** above a **classical serif** wordmark. That is a
brand about **power, protection, momentum, heritage, and human spirit** — not
cost-arbitrage call centers.

The market gap is precise. IntouchCX is brand-forward but broad and light on hard
proof. Hugo is proof-rich but wears a friendly, colorful, sans-serif "SaaS"
costume. **Neither owns _premium_.** Neither goes truly _deep_ in one vertical.
Both hedge the human-vs-AI question.

Invicta's winning position sits in the open space all three of those leave:

> **The Human Advantage — a premium, human-led customer-experience partner that
> defends brands, resolves complexity, and grows lifetime value. Proven in the
> most demanding arena in CX: high-stakes iGaming.**

Three strategic bets drive every recommendation in this document:

1. **Own "premium + human" visually and verbally** — editorial restraint,
   crimson-on-ink, serif headlines, real people. Look like Apple/Stripe/Linear
   discipline wearing Invicta's crimson, not like a friendly SaaS.
2. **Make Gaming the flagship credibility engine.** We have deep, specific,
   _true_ proof in iGaming (Lunaland: 4.5★ Trustpilot, <60s first response, 700+
   titles, 24/7/365). Lead with the hardest vertical; let its credibility carry
   into every other industry as "the same operating system, applied to you."
3. **Take the strongest honest stance on humans.** AI is internal-only
   augmentation; humans build trust, resolve complexity, retain customers, and
   protect the brand. This is our line in the sand and our biggest message.

## 1. Brand Positioning

**Positioning statement (internal):**
_For high-growth digital businesses — led by iGaming and sweepstakes operators —
Invicta is the premium customer-experience partner that deploys elite, rigorously
trained human teams to protect brands, resolve complex issues, and grow the
lifetime value of every customer. Unlike commodity BPOs that automate the human
out of support, Invicta **engineers** human excellence — recruitment, an
onboarding academy, continuous QA, workforce science, and security — so every
interaction compounds trust and revenue._

**Category we play in:** Premium Customer Experience (CX) partnership — not "BPO,"
not "call center," not "outsourcing." We use the word **partner**, not vendor.

**Brand platform / big idea:** **The Human Advantage.**
Endline tied to the mark: **_Invicta — Unconquered._**

**Why this is the right position:**

- **It is true and defensible.** The source document is overwhelmingly about
  _human_ operational excellence (academy, tone matrix, QA audits, VIP
  relationships). We are claiming what we actually are.
- **It exploits the exact gap competitors leave.** Premium + deep-vertical +
  unapologetically human is unoccupied territory between IntouchCX (broad brand)
  and Hugo (proof + price).
- **The brand assets already encode it.** A warhorse and a Latin word for
  "unconquered" cannot credibly sell "cheap seats." They sell _protection,
  resilience, and premium partnership_ — so the strategy aligns with the mark
  instead of fighting it.
- **It converts.** B2B buyers of CX fear one thing above price: a partner who
  damages the customer relationship. "We defend your brand with people who are
  trained like yours" is the highest-trust promise we can make.

**Brand personality:** Confident, composed, protective, precise, warm-but-serious.
The character of a champion, not a cheerleader. (Contrast: Hugo is playful;
IntouchCX is energetic; Invicta is _assured_.)

## 2. Messaging Strategy

**Message architecture (one primary, four pillars):**

- **Primary message (repeated site-wide):** _Humans build trust. We build the
  humans._ AI assists our people internally; it never replaces the person your
  customer talks to.
- **Pillar 1 — Human Expertise, Engineered.** Not scripts and turnover — a
  recruitment-to-academy-to-QA system that manufactures consistency. _Proof:_
  Onboarding Academy, "Player-First" tone matrix, weekly interaction audits.
- **Pillar 2 — Built for the Highest-Stakes Arena.** If we can run support for a
  sweepstakes casino with 700+ titles, real-money disputes, and 24/7 global
  traffic, your operation is well within our tolerance. _Proof:_ Lunaland.
- **Pillar 3 — Protection & Trust.** KYC, AML, fraud, risk, chargeback defense,
  age verification. We protect your integrity and your customers. _Proof:_
  multi-layer verification, fraud/bonus-abuse detection.
- **Pillar 4 — Support as a Growth Engine.** VIP management, retention, and
  reactivation turn a cost center into lifetime-value creation. _Proof:_ VIP
  squads, targeted re-engagement, LTV lift.

**Tone & voice principles:**

- **Claims are always backed by a number or a mechanism.** ("<60s first
  response," "weekly QA audits") — earned confidence, never hype.
- **Plain, editorial, senior.** Short declaratives. We sound like the smartest
  operator in the room, not a marketing brochure.
- **Protective, not boastful.** The hero of the story is the client's brand and
  customer; Invicta is the champion that defends them.

**Why:** A single, relentlessly repeated primary message is how challenger brands
win mindshare. Backing every claim with a mechanism/metric is the antidote to the
"all BPOs sound the same" problem and directly out-positions IntouchCX's
metric-light copy.

## 3. Target Audience

**Firmographics:** high-growth digital businesses, ~Series A through enterprise,
where customer interactions are frequent, emotionally charged, or compliance-heavy.

**Priority order (matches business priority):**

1. **iGaming / sweepstakes / social casino operators** — flagship. Highest pain,
   highest willingness to pay for specialists, our deepest proof.
2. **FinTech / crypto** — KYC/AML/fraud overlap with our gaming muscle.
3. **SaaS / Technology / Marketplaces** — scaling support, trust & safety.
4. **Ecommerce, Travel, Healthcare, Recruitment, Professional Services** —
   broad-market demand; served via the transferable operating model.

**Buying committee (who we design for):** Economic buyer (COO/CFO/Founder),
Champion (VP/Head of CX, Player Ops, Support), Technical/Compliance evaluator
(Risk/Fraud/Compliance lead), and often Procurement. The site must speak to all
four without diluting the emotional pitch to the champion.

**Why:** Naming the committee explicitly lets us place the right proof at the
right moment (emotional story for the champion; metrics, security, and pricing
transparency for the buyer and evaluator).

## 4. User Personas

1. **"The Scaling Operator" — Head of Player Operations / COO, iGaming (PRIMARY).**
   Drowning in ticket volume across hundreds of titles; terrified of a Trustpilot
   slide and compliance exposure; measured on CSAT, FRT, retention. _Wants:_ a
   specialist who already speaks sweepstakes and can stand up 24/7 fast.
   _Site job:_ prove depth instantly; show the Lunaland outcome.

2. **"The Brand Protector" — VP Customer Experience, SaaS/Marketplace.**
   Guards NPS and brand reputation; fears offshore quality drop and robotic
   support. _Wants:_ evidence our people are as good as an in-house team.
   _Site job:_ human-quality proof (academy, QA, tone), real faces.

3. **"The Compliance Owner" — Head of Risk / Fraud / Compliance, FinTech/Gaming.**
   Owns KYC/AML, chargebacks, fraud loss. _Wants:_ rigor, security posture,
   auditability. _Site job:_ Trust & Protection pillar, security/compliance page.

4. **"The Efficiency Executive" — CFO / COO.**
   Wants premium outcomes at a defensible cost; ROI and LTV thinking. _Wants:_
   transparent economics and the growth (not just cost) story. _Site job:_
   pricing-benchmark transparency, VIP/LTV narrative, "what's in every seat."

5. **"The Builder" — Founder / Ops lead, early-scale startup.**
   Needs a flexible partner who can start small and grow. _Wants:_ speed, low
   friction, trust. _Site job:_ clear "how it works," fast consultation CTA.

**Why:** Personas convert the abstract audience into concrete section-level
decisions (what proof, which CTA, what tone) and prevent designing only for the
champion while ignoring the people who sign.

## 6. Information Architecture

**Guiding principles:** (a) Gaming is the hero, not one tab among twelve. (b)
Reclassify cleanly: **Appointment Setting and Lead Generation are _services_, not
industries** — they are functions we perform, so filing them under Industries
would confuse the taxonomy and hurt SEO. (c) Keep the top nav to five decisions;
push depth into mega-menus.

**Top-level structure:**

- **Industries** (vertical depth) — Gaming & iGaming (flagship), FinTech &
  Crypto, SaaS & Technology, Ecommerce, Healthcare, Travel & Hospitality,
  Marketplaces, Recruitment & Staffing, Professional Services.
- **Services** (capability) — Customer Support (chat/email/voice/omnichannel),
  KYC & Compliance, Payments · Risk · Fraud (AML), VIP & Retention Management,
  Quality Assurance, Workforce Management, Trust & Safety / Content Moderation,
  Back Office, Lead Generation, Appointment Setting.
- **The Invicta Method** (how we operate — our moat) — Recruitment, Onboarding
  Academy, QA Engine, Workforce Science, Security & Compliance, Tech Stack.
- **Case Studies** — Lunaland flagship + a repeatable template.
- **Company** — About, Careers, Insights, Contact.
- **Legal** — Privacy, Terms (footer).

**Why:** A dedicated **Method** hub is the structural expression of our
positioning — it is the "engineered human excellence" proof that neither
competitor foregrounds, and it gives evaluators a place to get rigorous.

## 9. Homepage Story Flow

A cinematic, scroll-told narrative — problem → conviction → proof → depth →
invitation. Each block is one clear idea.

1. **Hero — the thesis.** Serif headline on the human advantage (e.g., "Customer
   experience, _championed_ by people."), one-line subhead, single CTA, a subtle
   crimson/ink ambient (or restrained motion). Establishes premium in 3 seconds.
2. **The proof band.** Live-feeling metrics: _4.5★ Trustpilot · <60s first
   response · 24/7/365 · 700+ titles supported._ Immediate, specific credibility.
3. **The tension.** The modern CX failure: automation-first support erodes trust
   and retention. Names the enemy without naming competitors.
4. **The Invicta answer.** Human-led, engineered excellence — the primary message.
5. **Capabilities.** The service set, scannable, each a doorway.
6. **Flagship spotlight — Gaming.** A high-drama teaser that pulls to the Gaming
   page. Signals "specialists, not generalists."
7. **The Invicta Method.** Four-step operating system (Recruit → Academy → QA →
   WFM). Our moat, made tangible.
8. **Case study — Lunaland.** The outcome story with real metrics.
9. **Trust & Protection.** KYC/AML/fraud/security — for the compliance evaluator.
10. **Global delivery.** 24/7/365 cross-border coverage.
11. **Voice of the customer.** Sentiment/testimonial (drawn from the documented
    "overwhelmingly positive support reviews").
12. **The invitation (CTA).** "Become an extension of your company." One human,
    low-friction next step.
13. **Footer.**

**Why:** This arc is engineered for the skeptical B2B buyer — hook with premium,
prove fast, dramatize depth, de-risk with method + security, then invite. It's a
persuasion sequence, not a feature list.

## 11. Industry Page Strategy

**Template:** Industry-specific pain → why generic BPOs fail this vertical →
Invicta's tailored operating model → the services that matter here → proof (or,
where we lack a case study, the transferable Method + relevant capability) → CTA.

**Integrity rule:** We have _one_ documented case study (gaming). For industries
without proof, we **do not fabricate logos or metrics.** We lead with the
transferable operating system ("the same rigor that sustains a 4.5★ sweepstakes
casino, applied to your world") and clearly-labeled capability content. This
protects trust — the entire brand promise.

**Why:** Honest, capability-led industry pages still rank and convert, and they
avoid the credibility landmine of implied-but-unproven claims — fatal for a
brand whose core promise _is_ trust.

## 12. Gaming Page Strategy (Flagship)

The most immersive page on the site — the one we over-invest in. It must _feel_
like the intensity of a live 24/7 gaming operation while staying fast and
accessible.

**Narrative & signature moments:**

- **Immersive hero:** the only page that may use a restrained WebGL/3D or

- **The stakes:** 700+ titles, real-money disputes, sweepstakes compliance, VIP
  economics — dramatized with scroll.
- **Live-support visualization:** a "<60 seconds" first-response moment made
  visceral (animated timer / chat cadence).
- **Tier-support matrix:** an animated escalation flow (Tier 1 → specialists).
- **KYC / fraud defense:** a "guardian" sequence — the protection narrative at
  full volume.
- **VIP journey:** how a high-value player is nurtured to higher LTV.
- **The Lunaland outcome:** the metrics, presented as the payoff.
- **Compliance & security** reassurance, then a specialist CTA.

**Why:** Gaming is where our proof is real and our buyer pays the premium. Making
it the most cinematic page concentrates our best design/motion effort where it
has the highest commercial return, and it becomes the reference that sells every
other vertical.

## 14. CTA Strategy

- **Primary (one, everywhere):** **"Talk to an Expert"** / **"Book a
  Consultation"** → a high-touch, human, calendar-based flow. Consultative, not
  transactional — it matches a considered B2B purchase.
- **Secondary (contextual):** "See the Lunaland story," "Explore the Method,"
  "Build your team."
- **Placement:** sticky header CTA; one mid-page CTA per major section transition;
  a full-bleed closing CTA on every page. Never competing primary CTAs on one
  screen.
- **Micro-conversions:** newsletter/insights, gated playbook (see §16).

**Why:** A single, consistent, human primary CTA compounds recognition and
reduces decision fatigue — a direct correction of the diffuse multi-CTA pattern
seen on competitor sites.

## 15. Trust-Building Strategy

Layered proof, placed exactly where doubt peaks:

- **Hard metrics** (4.5★, <60s, 24/7/365, 700+) in the hero band and repeated.
- **The Method** — visible operational rigor (academy, QA audits, WFM).
- **Security & compliance** — a dedicated posture: KYC/AML, data security, VPN/

- **Radical economic transparency** — publish the FTE inclusions and benchmark
  ranges from the source doc. Almost no competitor shows real pricing; doing so is
  a disproportionate trust signal.
- **Real people** — authentic photography of actual teams (see §23).
- **Voice of customer** — documented positive-sentiment quotes.

**Why:** In CX outsourcing the sale is trust, and trust is built by _specific,
verifiable, and unusually transparent_ signals. Pricing transparency in
particular is a low-cost, high-impact differentiator.

## 17. Design System Recommendations

**Design language: "Editorial Precision."** Apple/Stripe/Linear structural
discipline (grids, whitespace, restraint) + a heritage-editorial soul (serif
display, crimson accent, real photography). Premium, timeless, confident.

- **Token architecture (3 layers):** primitives (raw values) → semantic tokens
  (`--color-brand`, `--color-ink`, `--space-section`) → component tokens. Built on
  the existing Tailwind v4 `@theme` + shadcn setup from the foundation.
- **Grid:** 12-col, max-width ~1280–1440, wide gutters, an 8px spacing base with a
  deliberate large-step scale for editorial breathing room.
- **Elevation & surface:** mostly flat, high-contrast; sparing use of glass and
  soft shadow; crimson reserved for meaning (interaction, emphasis, brand
  moments), never as wallpaper.
- **Two intensities:** a calm, editorial system for most pages; a heightened,
  immersive layer (3D/advanced motion) unlocked only for Gaming and hero moments.

**Why:** A disciplined token system is what makes 30+ pages feel like one brand
and keeps Phases 3–7 fast and consistent; "editorial precision" is the visual
translation of "premium + human + unconquered."

## 18. Typography Recommendations

**Pairing (duality of the brand):**

- **Display / headlines — a refined serif** that echoes the logo's classical
  wordmark and injects human warmth + editorial gravitas. Recommended:
  **Fraunces** (variable, self-hostable, characterful old-style) or a cleaner
  alternative (**Newsreader** / a licensed high-contrast serif). This is the
  "heritage, human, unconquered" voice.
- **Body / UI — a precise neutral grotesk.** Keep **Geist** (already in the
  foundation) or **Inter**. This is the "operational precision, modern" voice.
- **Mono (data/metrics)** — Geist Mono for KPIs and technical labels, reinforcing
  the "engineered" credibility.

- **Fluid type scale** (already scaffolded via `clamp()`), large editorial
  headline sizes, tight display tracking, generous line-height on body.

**Why:** A serif-display + grotesk-body pairing is _on-brand by construction_ (the
logo is a serif), differentiates sharply from the all-sans look of both
competitors, and literally expresses the brand duality: human warmth × operational
precision.

## 19. Color System

Derived from the mark: **crimson is the brand spark, used with luxury restraint on
an ink-and-ivory editorial canvas.**

- **Brand — Invicta Crimson:** the horse red (~`#B4121B`/oklch equivalent), with a
  deeper **oxblood** for premium depth and a brighter tint for interactive
  accents. Used sparingly — CTAs, emphasis, brand moments, gaming intensity.
- **Ink:** a rich near-black (warm-leaning, not pure `#000`) as the dominant
  text/base — the source of the premium, editorial feel.
- **Ivory / warm off-white:** primary light background — warmer than clinical
  white to echo _human_, not _corporate_.
- **Warm neutral grey scale:** slightly warm greys (never cold blue-greys) for UI,
  borders, muted text — reinforcing humanity.
- **Support/functional:** success/war, plus a restrained cool tone _only_ for
  data-viz contrast if needed.
- **Dark mode / immersive:** near-black canvas with crimson energy — the natural
  register for the Gaming flagship and hero moments.

**Discipline rule:** red is a _scalpel, not a bucket._ Predominantly monochrome
(ink on ivory) with crimson as the recurring signature keeps it premium and avoids
"casino red" cheapness.

**Why:** Grounding the palette in the real mark makes the brand cohesive from logo
to pixel; restraint is precisely what separates "premium" from "loud," and the
crimson-on-ink editorial canvas is unoccupied space vs. competitors' colorful,
approachable palettes.

## 21. Motion Language

**Principle: motion carries meaning, never decoration.** It reveals hierarchy,
guides the eye, and rewards scrolling.

- **Signature behaviors:** masked serif text reveals (GSAP SplitText), editorial
  image mask-reveals, section-anchored scroll timelines (ScrollTrigger), magnetic
  primary CTAs, subtle parallax depth, restrained marquees for logos/values.
- **Smoothness:** Lenis-driven scroll (already wired) for a premium, weighty feel.
- **Tiered intensity:** _Editorial tier_ (most pages) — quiet, confident,
  fast. _Immersive tier_ (Gaming + hero moments) — WebGL/3D, particle/energy,
  pinned scroll set-pieces.
- **Choreography:** consistent easing/duration/stagger tokens (already
  scaffolded) so motion feels like one authored hand across the site.

**Why:** Purposeful, tokenized motion is what makes a site feel "award-worthy"
rather than gimmicky, and reserving heavy motion for the flagship protects both
performance and meaning.

---

## 22. Animation Philosophy

- **Restraint is premium.** Every animation must justify itself; if it doesn't add
  clarity or delight, it's cut.
- **Performance is a feature.** 60fps target; GPU-friendly transforms only;
  defer/lazy-load 3D; never block LCP with motion.
- **Accessibility is non-negotiable.** Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support
  (already built into the foundation) — motion degrades to instant/opacity.
- **Consistency over novelty.** A small, repeated motion vocabulary beats a
  scrapbook of effects.

**Why:** These four rules are what separate the top 1% of animated sites (fast,
tasteful, accessible) from the impressive-but-broken majority — directly serving
the "one of the best experiences on the internet" bar.

## 23. Photography Direction

**Real humans, treated like an editorial magazine — because "human" is the whole
message.**

- **Subjects:** actual Invicta agents, team leads, VIP managers, QA — captured
  authentically at work; global and diverse; warm, confident, competent.
- **Style:** documentary-editorial, natural light, shallow depth, candid over
  posed; a consistent warm-leaning color grade with crimson accents in-frame where
  natural.
- **Avoid:** generic stock, headset-cliché call-center imagery, cold corporate.
- **Interim plan:** until a branded shoot exists, use carefully-selected,
  consistently-graded authentic imagery and abstract crimson/ink art for
  tech/gaming — **never** stocky "smiling headset" tropes that undercut premium.

**Why:** If the differentiator is people, the photography _is_ the proof. Editorial
human imagery is also the fastest way to look premium and unlike both competitors'
illustration-led looks.

## 24. Video Strategy

- **Hero ambient:** short, muted, subtle loop (agents/ops or abstract crimson
  energy) with a poster frame; performance-guarded, never autoplay-with-sound.
- **Case-study film:** a Lunaland-style outcome story (interim: motion-typography
  - stills if no footage yet).
- **Method micro-films:** short clips for academy/QA/WFM.
- **Delivery:** lazy-loaded, `LazyMount`/`ClientOnly` (already built), adaptive
  quality, poster-first, reduced-motion respected.

**Why:** Video massively raises perceived premium and dwell time, but only if it
never harms Core Web Vitals — hence the strict lazy/poster/guard rules.

## 25. SEO Strategy

- **Architecture as SEO:** the Industry × Service matrix is a programmatic
  landing-page engine (e.g., "iGaming customer support outsourcing," "KYC
  verification services," "VIP player management," "sweepstakes support"). Clean,
  flat, keyword-aligned URLs (see §7).
- **Topic clusters:** Insights hub anchored by pillar pages (Gaming CX, KYC/AML,
  Retention/VIP) with supporting articles linking inward.
- **Technical:** Next.js SSG/ISR, per-page metadata (already scaffolded via
  `createMetadata`), structured data (Organization, Service, BreadcrumbList, FAQ,
  Article), XML sitemaps, canonicals, fast CWV as a ranking asset.
- **Intent match:** target high-commercial-intent, specialist long-tail where we
  can rank and convert, rather than fighting incumbents on generic "BPO."

**Why:** Deep vertical specificity is both our brand position _and_ our SEO
advantage — it's easier to rank and convert on "iGaming VIP retention support"
than on "customer service outsourcing," and it reinforces the specialist story.

## 26. Accessibility Strategy

- **Target: WCAG 2.2 AA.** Semantic HTML, logical heading order, full keyboard
  operability, visible focus states, ARIA only where needed (menus, dialogs).
- **Contrast:** crimson-on-ivory and text combinations validated ≥4.5:1 (a real
  constraint the color system must respect — informs which red shades carry text).
- **Motion & media:** `prefers-reduced-motion` throughout (built), captions/
  transcripts for video, no motion-only meaning.
- **Forms:** labels, errors, and instructions programmatically associated.
- **Process:** automated (axe/Lighthouse) + manual keyboard/SR audits in Phase 7.

**Why:** For an _enterprise_ brand, accessibility is both an ethical baseline and
a procurement requirement; buyers' legal/compliance teams check it. It also
overlaps heavily with SEO and quality.

## 27. Performance Strategy

- **Targets:** Lighthouse 95+; **LCP < 2.5s, INP < 200ms, CLS < 0.1** (field +
  lab).
- **Levers (mostly pre-wired in the foundation):** SSG/ISR, image optimization
  (AVIF/WebP, `next/image`, blur-up), font strategy (self-hosted, preloaded,
  size-adjusted fallbacks — no CLS), route-level code splitting, `optimizePackage
Imports`, deferred/lazy 3D and video, edge/CDN delivery.
- **Budgets:** per-route JS budget; heavy libraries (three/R3F) loaded _only_ on
  the Gaming flagship via dynamic import + `LazyMount`.
- **Monitoring:** `WebVitals` reporter (built) → analytics; performance checked
  every phase, not just at the end.

**Why:** On a motion-heavy premium site, performance is the difference between
"award-worthy" and "beautiful but janky." Enforcing budgets continuously (and
quarantining 3D to one route) is how we keep 95+ while still being cinematic.

## 28. Mobile Strategy

- **Mobile-first, not mobile-also.** The majority of B2B research traffic is
  mobile; the story must land on a phone first.
- **Adaptations:** condensed hero and copy, single-column editorial rhythm,
  thumb-reachable sticky CTA, large serif tap targets (≥44px), simplified motion,
  and 3D reduced or replaced with a lightweight fallback on low-power devices.
- **Performance priority:** even stricter budgets on mobile; ambient video/3D
  gated by device capability and reduced-motion.

**Why:** A premium desktop experience that stumbles on mobile fails the exact
moment most first impressions happen; designing the constrained case first
guarantees the story survives everywhere.

## 29. Conversion Strategy

- **Above-the-fold clarity:** who/what/why-premium in one screen.
- **Proximity of proof to asks:** metrics and security beside every CTA.
- **Friction reduction:** short qualifying forms, calendar booking, no dead ends.
- **One primary CTA, everywhere** (see §14); persistent sticky access.
- **Speed as conversion:** every 100ms matters; performance budget _is_ CRO.
- **Trust density at decision points:** pricing transparency + "what's in a seat"
  right where cost anxiety peaks.
- **Measurement:** event tracking on CTAs, scroll-depth, form steps; iterate.

**Why:** In considered B2B, conversion is won by _removing doubt and friction_ at
the exact moments they arise — not by aggressive tactics. Every recommendation
above lowers a specific barrier for a specific persona.
