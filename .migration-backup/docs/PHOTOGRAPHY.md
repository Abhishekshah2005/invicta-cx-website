# Invicta — Photography Direction & Shot List

The homepage's emotional weight rests on its imagery. Every `ImagePlaceholder`
on the site carries a caption that IS the shot spec; this document is the master
brief. When real assets arrive, swap each `ImagePlaceholder` for `<BlurImage>`
at the same aspect ratio.

## Art direction (applies to every shot)

- **Real people, real work.** Actual specialists, team leads, operations,
  customer-success and leadership — never models, never stock.
- **No clichés.** No headset-and-thumbs-up, no fake wide grins, no rows of
  identical cubicles, no glowing "AI" holograms.
- **Cinematic, editorial, warm.** Natural or motivated light, shallow depth of
  field, filmic grade with lifted blacks and a subtle warm cast. Crimson only
  where it occurs naturally (a lanyard, a screen, ambient light).
- **Candid over posed.** Mid-conversation, mid-thought, mid-resolution — the
  moment a problem becomes trust.
- **Diverse and global.** Reflect a genuine cross-border, multicultural team.
- **Format:** deliver 4:5 and 4:3 masters, high-res, colour-managed (Display P3
  → sRGB export). Leave clean negative space for type where noted.

## Sourcing priority

1. **Commission a branded shoot** of the real Invicta team and floor (ideal —
   the differentiator is the people, so the people should be in the frame).
2. Interim: a tightly art-directed, consistently-graded selection of authentic
   documentary imagery — never the generic call-center stock look.
3. For industries without an on-site shoot, use restrained, on-tone editorial
   imagery graded to match, or hold the branded `ImagePlaceholder`.
